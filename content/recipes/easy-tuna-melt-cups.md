**Easy Tuna Melt Cups** are the ultimate solution for hectic weeknights when you crave something hearty yet hassle-free. This recipe reimagines the classic tuna melt with a fun, portable twist—perfect for families, meal prep, or even lunchboxes. Packed with protein, customizable with veggies, and baked to golden perfection, these cups are a crowd-pleaser that’s as easy as opening a can. Whether you’re feeding picky eaters or just want a 30-minute dinner that disappears off the plate, this **Easy Tuna Melt Cups recipe** will become your go-to.  

## Why You'll Love This Recipe  
- **Lightning-fast prep**: No chopping, no fuss—just mix, stuff, and bake.  
- **Kid-approved**: The melty cheese and savory tuna combo wins over even the pickiest eaters.  
- **Low-carb friendly**: Skip the bread or use cauliflower for a keto-friendly version.  
- **Versatile twist**: Add bell peppers, spinach, or avocado for extra flavor and nutrition.  
- **Meal prep dream**: Make a batch ahead and freeze for quick reheating.  

## Ingredients  
- 2 (5 oz) cans chunk light tuna, drained  
- 4 slices whole-grain bread, crusts trimmed  
- 1 cup shredded cheddar or mozzarella cheese  
- 2 tbsp mayonnaise  
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard  
- 1/2 tsp garlic powder  
- 1/2 tsp onion powder  
- 1/4 tsp smoked paprika (optional)  
- Pinch of salt and black pepper  
- Optional: 1/2 cup chopped bell peppers, spinach, or cooked broccoli  

## Instructions  
1. **Preheat oven** to 375°F (190°C). Grease a muffin tin with non-stick spray.  
2. **Toast bread slices** in a toaster or skillet until golden brown. Set aside.  
3. **Mix tuna filling**: In a bowl, combine drained tuna, mayo, mustard, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, salt, and pepper. Fold in optional veggies if using.  
4. **Stuff the cups**: Spoon tuna mixture into each bread slice, pressing gently to pack. Top with 2 tbsp shredded cheese per cup.  
5. **Bake**: Transfer muffin tin to the oven and bake for 15-20 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.  
6. **Serve immediately**: Garnish with a sprinkle of paprika or a lemon wedge for brightness.  

## Pro Tips  
- **Bread hack**: Use garlic bread or sourdough for extra flavor without extra steps.  
- **Veggie boost**: Sauté spinach or diced peppers first to soften them before adding to the mix.  
- **Make-ahead magic**: Assemble the cups ahead and refrigerate for up to 24 hours before baking.  

## Storage & Leftovers  
Store leftover tuna melt cups in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days. Reheat in the oven at 350°F (175°C) for 10 minutes to retain crispiness.
